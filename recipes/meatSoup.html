Meat Soup

Description

This hearty and flavorful Viking meat soup is made using traditional cooking methods and tools, such as hand-forged iron knives, an open flame, and an iron cauldron or cooking pot. The use of ingredients such as venison, parsnips, and turnips reflects the dietary habits of the Viking Age, while the spices and herbs add depth and complexity to the flavor profile of the soup.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of boar or other game meat
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 parsnips, diced
  • 1 turnip, diced
  • 2 horns full of water
  • 1 smidgen of sea salt
  • hand full of stinging nettle leaves
  • 1 wooden spoon of rendered animal fat (such as tallow)

Steps

  1. Cut the boar or other game meat into small pieces using a hand-forged iron knife.
  2. In a large iron cauldron or cooking pot, heat the rendered animal fat over an open flame.
  3. Add the diced onion to the pot, and sauté it until it is golden brown.
  4. Add the diced carrots, parsnips, and turnip to the pot, and sauté them for a few minutes until they begin to soften.
  5. Add the boar or other game meat to the pot, and sauté it for a few minutes until it is browned on all sides.
  6. Add the water, sea salt, and stinging nettle leaves to the pot, and stir everything together with a wooden spoon.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil over the open flame, then remove the cauldron from the flame and let it simmer next to the fire.
  8. Cover the pot with a tightly-fitting iron lid, and let the soup simmer for 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meat and vegetables are tender.
  9. Serve the soup hot in wooden bowls.
