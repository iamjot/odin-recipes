Viking Porridge

Description

The Vikings had several options, when it came to making porridge. It could be made from barley, oats, buckwheat or millet. They mixed berries and apples into the porridge to add sweetness. Porridge was typically part of the daily food intake, especially that of the poor.

reference

Ingredients

  • One heaping handful of barley flour
  • One helmet full of water
  • One helmet full of goat's milk
  • Two apples
  • One wooden spoon full of honey
  • One smidgen of sea salt

    • Steps

    1. In a large iron cauldron or cooking pot, combine the barley flour, water, goat's milk, and sea salt.
    2. Place the cauldron over a roaring fire, and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring frequently with a long wooden spoon.
    3. With a broad sword or battle axe finely chop the apples into small bite sized pieces.
    4. Remove the cauldron from the fire and place it next to the fire. stir in the apples and occassionally stir the porridge with the long wooden spoon until it thickens to your desired texture.
    5. Scoop the porridge into a warm wooden bowl and stir in the honey.
    6. Add some chopped nuts or more goat's milk if desired
    7. Enjoy!
