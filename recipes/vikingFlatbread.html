Viking Flatbread

Description

This simple and hearty Viking-style breakfast would have been cooked over an open flame using iron cauldrons and wooden utensils, and served on wooden bowls and platters. The use of ingredients such as goat's milk, barley flour, and rye flour reflects the dietary habits of the Viking Age, while the cooking techniques and utensils reflect the cultural traditions of the time.

Ingredients

  • 1 hand full of barley flour
  • 1 hand full of rye flour
  • 1 small wooden spoonful of baking powder (made from ground seashells)
  • 1 smidgen of sea salt
  • 1 horn full of water
  • 1/4 horn of melted butter (made from unicorn's milk)

Steps

  1. In a large wooden bowl, whisk together the barley flour, rye flour, baking powder (made from ground seashells), and sea salt.
  2. Add the water and melted butter (made from unicorn's milk), and stir the mixture together with a wooden spoon, forming a dough.
  3. Place the dough on a flat, smooth surface, and use a large smooth log to roll it out into a large, flat disc.
  4. Heat a large, flat griddle or skillet over an open fire, and place the flatbread onto the hot surface.
  5. Cook the flatbread for 2-3 minutes on each side, until it is lightly browned and cooked through.
  6. Remove the flatbread from the griddle or skillet, and serve it hot on wooden platters.
